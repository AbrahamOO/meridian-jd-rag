# Customer Complaint Handling Procedure

## Purpose and Scope

This procedure defines how Meridian John Doe Financial (Meridian J.D.) receives, records, acknowledges, investigates, resolves, and reports customer complaints. It ensures every complaint is captured at the point of contact, acknowledged within a firm timeline, routed to the right resolver, escalated when it carries regulatory or reputational risk, and closed with a documented outcome. It protects the customer, surfaces systemic issues, and produces the complaint record the bank relies on for regulatory examination and fair-treatment monitoring.

This procedure applies to:

- All retail channels: branch floor, contact center, digital banking, mail, and complaints received through regulators or third parties.
- All Branch Staff who receive a complaint, the Branch Manager, Operations Analysts who manage the complaint queue and investigate, and Compliance Officers who oversee regulatory complaints and fair-treatment risk.
- All product complaints originating from retail customers, including deposit, payments, and consumer lending serviced through the branch network.

This procedure governs complaint intake, timelines, and reporting. Where a complaint is also a Regulation E electronic-transaction error claim or a chargeback, the investigation mechanics are owned by MJD-OPS-0006 Dispute and Chargeback Resolution Procedure and the regulatory error-resolution clock is owned by MJD-CMP-0006 Regulation E Error Resolution Procedure; this procedure tracks the complaint envelope around those processes so nothing falls between them. Fair-lending and discrimination concerns are co-owned with Compliance under MJD-CMP-0007.

## Definitions

- **Complaint.** Any expression of dissatisfaction by or on behalf of a customer about a Meridian J.D. product, service, employee, or process, whether spoken, written, or received through a regulator, regardless of whether the customer uses the word complaint.
- **Inquiry.** A request for information or routine service that is satisfied at first contact and does not express dissatisfaction. An inquiry that cannot be resolved and leaves the customer dissatisfied becomes a complaint.
- **Regulatory Complaint.** A complaint received from or referred by a regulator (for example the CFPB complaint portal) or a complaint alleging a legal or regulatory violation.
- **Escalated Complaint.** A complaint that meets a severity trigger in Section 4 and is routed to Compliance or senior management.
- **Acknowledgement.** The bank's first substantive response to the customer confirming the complaint was received and is being handled.
- **Resolution.** A documented outcome communicated to the customer that addresses the complaint, whether the bank agrees, partially agrees, or declines, with the reason.
- **UDAAP.** Unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts or practices, a standard against which complaint themes are monitored.

## 1. Complaint Intake

### 1.1 Recognizing a complaint

1.1.1 Any employee who receives an expression of dissatisfaction logs it as a complaint. Staff do not screen out a grievance because they believe it is unfounded; the log captures the customer's assertion, and the investigation tests it.

1.1.2 A complaint is recognized regardless of channel or wording. A customer who says "this is the third time you charged me wrongly" has complained even without using the word.

### 1.2 Logging

1.2.1 The receiving employee records the complaint in the complaint management system at the point of contact, or before end of business day for a written or indirect complaint. The record captures: customer identity, date and channel received, the complaint as stated, the product or process involved, and the receiving employee.

1.2.2 Every complaint receives a unique complaint reference number used in all customer communication and internal handoffs.

1.2.3 Complaint records contain customer personal information and are handled at the INTERNAL classification under MJD-SEC-0008 Data Classification and Handling Standard. They are not shared outside the resolution path.

1.2.4 A complaint received in writing (letter, email, secure message, or a regulator referral) is logged the same business day it is received, even if it cannot be substantively answered that day. The logging date, not the date staff begin work, starts the acknowledgement and resolution clocks.

1.2.5 A complaint made on behalf of a customer by an authorized representative, an attorney, an advocate, or a family member with authority is logged as the customer's complaint. The branch verifies the representative's authority before disclosing account detail to them, but never delays logging the complaint while authority is being verified.

### 1.3 Categorization

1.3.1 The receiving employee assigns a preliminary category and a preliminary severity. Operations confirms or corrects the categorization at triage in Section 2.

1.3.2 The standard complaint categories are:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Fees and charges | Disputed fee, fee not disclosed, fee reversal request |
| Account access | Locked account, card not working, online access failure |
| Electronic transaction error | Unauthorized charge, duplicate debit, ATM not dispensing |
| Payments and transfers | Wire delay, misdirected transfer, bill-pay failure |
| Lending decision | Denial, terms, servicing, alleged discrimination |
| Staff conduct | Rudeness, error, alleged misconduct involving funds |
| Privacy and data | Suspected breach, misdirected statement, data accuracy |
| Disclosure and marketing | Misleading statement, unclear terms, potential UDAAP |

1.3.3 Category and severity are working classifications. The owner updates them as facts develop; a complaint that began as a fee dispute and reveals a possible privacy breach is reclassified to the higher severity and re-routed under Section 4.

## 2. Acknowledgement and Triage

### 2.1 Acknowledgement timeline

2.1.1 Every complaint is acknowledged to the customer within **five business days** of receipt. A complaint resolved at first contact is acknowledged and closed in the same interaction, recorded as a same-day resolution.

2.1.2 The acknowledgement confirms the complaint reference number, that the bank is investigating, and the expected resolution timeframe. It does not promise a specific outcome.

### 2.2 Triage and ownership

2.2.1 Operations triages each open complaint within **two business days** of logging, confirms the category and severity, and assigns an owner. A branch-resolvable complaint is owned by the Branch Manager; a complex or cross-channel complaint is owned by an Operations Analyst.

2.2.2 At triage, Operations applies the severity rules in Section 4 and routes any complaint meeting an escalation trigger to Compliance the same business day.

### 2.3 Linkage to regulatory processes

2.3.1 If the complaint is an unauthorized or erroneous electronic transaction claim, it is linked to a Regulation E error case under MJD-CMP-0006 and the dispute mechanics under MJD-OPS-0006. The Regulation E investigation clock (generally up to 10 business days to provisionally resolve, extendable to 45 calendar days with provisional credit, and up to 90 calendar days for certain new-account or foreign or point-of-sale transactions) runs in that process; this complaint record references the linked case and does not restart or override that clock.

2.3.2 If the complaint concerns a credit decision and alleges discrimination, it is escalated to Compliance for fair-lending review under MJD-CMP-0007 Fair Lending (ECOA / Reg B) Policy.

2.3.3 The complaint envelope and the linked regulatory case run in parallel and are cross-referenced. The complaint is not closed until the linked regulatory case is resolved or until the complaint-specific dissatisfaction (which may be broader than the regulatory claim, for example the customer is unhappy with how staff handled the dispute) is addressed and communicated.

### 2.4 Timeline summary

| Step | Target |
|---|---|
| Log a complaint | Same business day received |
| Triage and assign owner | Within 2 business days of logging |
| Acknowledge the customer | Within 5 business days of receipt |
| Resolve and communicate (standard) | Within 30 calendar days of receipt |
| Holding response if delayed | Before day 30 |
| Resolve (extended cap) | No later than 45 calendar days, unless a linked statutory window controls |
| Tier 3 escalation to Compliance | Same business day |

The 5-business-day acknowledgement, the 2-business-day triage, and the 30-calendar-day resolution targets are firm controls measured and reported under Section 5. A linked Regulation E or other statutory clock controls its own portion and is never shortened or overridden by these targets.

## 3. Investigation and Resolution

### 3.1 Investigation

3.1.1 The complaint owner investigates the facts: reviews the account and transaction history, interviews the involved staff where conduct is alleged, and gathers documentation. The investigation is recorded in the complaint record with dates and findings.

3.1.2 The owner determines whether the complaint is upheld, partially upheld, or not upheld, and what remedy, if any, applies (for example a fee reversal, an apology, a process correction, or a referral for restitution).

3.1.3 The investigation is proportionate to severity. A Tier 1 fee dispute may need only a transaction review and a quick decision; a Tier 3 conduct or discrimination allegation needs a documented, independent review, often led by Compliance, with the involved employee not investigating their own conduct.

3.1.4 Where a complaint alleges an error that also affected other customers (for example a fee applied in error by a system rule), the owner raises a potential systemic issue under Section 5 so the population is identified and remediated, not just the single complainant.

### 3.2 Remedies

3.2.1 A remedy makes the customer whole where the bank is at fault: reversal of an erroneous fee or charge, credit of incorrectly debited funds, correction of a record, or a documented apology. A monetary remedy is recorded against the complaint with the amount and the approver under the override authority in MJD-RET-0001 Branch Operations Manual Section 5.4.

3.2.2 A goodwill gesture offered where the bank is not strictly at fault is allowed within delegated authority and is recorded as goodwill, distinct from an error correction, so reporting can distinguish fault from goodwill.

3.2.3 A remedy is never conditioned on the customer waiving the right to complain to a regulator or to pursue the matter further. Staff never ask a customer to drop a regulator complaint in exchange for a remedy.

### 3.3 Resolution timeline

3.3.1 The bank resolves and communicates the outcome of a standard complaint within **30 calendar days** of receipt.

3.3.2 If a complaint cannot be resolved within 30 calendar days, the owner sends the customer a holding response before day 30 explaining the delay and the new expected date, and resolves it no later than **45 calendar days** from receipt except where a linked regulatory process (such as Regulation E) sets a longer statutory window, in which case that window controls for the linked portion.

3.3.3 Regulatory complaints (for example CFPB portal complaints) are resolved within the timeframe set by the regulator's process, which for the CFPB portal is generally a response within 15 calendar days, with a final response within 60 calendar days. Compliance owns the response to the regulator.

### 3.4 Resolution communication

3.4.1 The outcome is communicated to the customer in the channel the customer prefers, in plain language, stating the decision, the reason, any remedy, and how to pursue the matter further if still dissatisfied.

3.4.2 The complaint record is closed only after the outcome is communicated and documented. A closed complaint that the customer reopens is relinked, not duplicated.

3.4.3 The closure record captures: the outcome (upheld, partially upheld, not upheld), the remedy if any, the root cause if identified, the communication date and channel, and the owner. A complaint cannot be closed in the system without these fields, so the bank cannot quietly close complaints without a documented outcome.

### 3.5 Worked example: a disputed overdraft fee

A customer comes to the branch upset that two overdraft fees were charged on the same day. The personal banker logs the complaint that day with the customer's account, the fee details, and the category "fees and charges," and gives the customer the complaint reference number and a same-day acknowledgement. Operations triages it the next business day as Tier 1. The owner reviews the posting order and finds one fee was correct and one resulted from a known timing issue. The bank reverses the one incorrect fee (an error correction, recorded with the approver under MJD-RET-0001 Section 5.4) and explains the correct fee in plain language. Because the timing issue may have affected other customers, the owner flags a potential systemic issue under Section 5. The outcome is communicated within the 30-calendar-day target and the complaint is closed with a documented outcome and root cause.

## 4. Severity and Escalation

### 4.1 Severity tiers

| Tier | Definition | Acknowledge | Resolve target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (low) | Routine service dissatisfaction, no regulatory angle, resolvable at the branch | Within 5 business days | Within 30 calendar days |
| Tier 2 (elevated) | Repeat complaint, fee or money impact, cross-channel, or potential process defect | Within 5 business days | Within 30 calendar days, Compliance informed |
| Tier 3 (high) | Alleges legal or regulatory violation, discrimination, UDAAP, privacy breach, or significant harm; or a regulatory complaint | Within 2 business days | Compliance-owned, per regulator timeline |

### 4.2 Mandatory escalation triggers

A complaint is escalated to Compliance the same business day if it: alleges discrimination or a fair-lending concern; alleges a privacy or data breach; alleges a UDAAP practice; is received through a regulator; involves potential elder financial exploitation; or names possible employee misconduct involving customer funds. Branch and Operations never close a Tier 3 complaint without Compliance sign-off.

### 4.3 Escalation path

4.3.1 Branch Staff escalate to the Branch Manager. The Branch Manager escalates Tier 2 and Tier 3 matters to Operations and, for Tier 3, to Compliance.

4.3.2 Compliance determines whether a complaint indicates reportable conduct, a Suspicious Activity Report referral under MJD-CMP-0002 Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) Filing Procedure, or a fraud referral under MJD-RSK-0007 Fraud Risk Management Procedure.

## 5. Reporting and Trend Monitoring

### 5.1 Complaint reporting

5.1.1 Operations produces a monthly complaint report by category, channel, severity, time-to-acknowledge, and time-to-resolve. Breaches of the five-business-day acknowledgement or the 30-calendar-day resolution target are flagged.

5.1.2 Compliance receives the report and monitors complaint themes for UDAAP and fair-treatment risk, escalating systemic issues to the appropriate risk committee.

5.1.3 The monthly report includes timeliness metrics: the percentage of complaints acknowledged within 5 business days, triaged within 2 business days, and resolved within 30 calendar days. A target-breach rate above the control threshold is itself escalated as a process issue.

### 5.2 Root cause and remediation

5.2.1 A category or theme exceeding its control threshold triggers a root-cause review. Where a process defect is found, the owner of the affected process implements a correction and the complaint report tracks the fix to closure.

5.2.2 A systemic issue affecting multiple customers triggers identification of the full affected population and proactive remediation, not just resolution of the customers who happened to complain. Compliance oversees remediation where the issue carries regulatory or UDAAP risk.

### 5.3 Record retention

Complaint records are retained per the bank's records schedule referenced in MJD-SEC-0008 Data Classification and Handling Standard and the bank-wide retention schedule. Complaint records are not deleted to clear a backlog or to avoid examination scrutiny; the no-suppression rule in the Exceptions section applies to deletion as well as to downgrading.

## 6. Fair Treatment and Vulnerable Customers

### 6.1 Fair and consistent handling

6.1.1 Complaints are handled fairly and consistently regardless of the customer's product value, tenure, or any protected characteristic. A complaint is never deprioritized because the customer is low-value or persistent, and never fast-tracked to silence a customer rather than to resolve the issue.

6.1.2 Complaint themes are monitored for disparate treatment. A pattern suggesting that a protected group experiences worse outcomes or slower handling is escalated to Compliance for fair-treatment review under MJD-CMP-0007 Fair Lending (ECOA / Reg B) Policy.

### 6.2 Vulnerable customers

6.2.1 A complaint from or about a customer who appears vulnerable (for example an older adult, a customer in financial distress, or a customer who may lack capacity) is handled with added care, in plain language, at the customer's pace, and with extra attention to possible exploitation.

6.2.2 Suspected elder or vulnerable-adult financial exploitation is a mandatory Tier 3 escalation to Compliance the same business day and may trigger a fraud referral under MJD-RSK-0007 Fraud Risk Management Procedure. The customer is protected first; staff do not tip off a suspected exploiter.

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Branch Staff | Recognize and log complaints at intake, acknowledge first contact, escalate to the Branch Manager |
| Branch Manager | Own Tier 1 complaints, escalate Tier 2 and Tier 3, ensure acknowledgement and resolution timelines are met |
| Operations Analyst | Triage and assign complaints, investigate complex cases, produce the monthly complaint report |
| Compliance Officer | Own Tier 3 and regulatory complaints, monitor UDAAP and fair-treatment themes, make SAR and fair-lending determinations |
| Head of Retail Banking | Own this procedure, report complaint metrics, sponsor remediation of systemic issues |

## Exceptions and Escalation

- An extension beyond the 30-calendar-day resolution target requires a documented holding response to the customer before day 30 and an owner-recorded reason; resolution still targets 45 calendar days unless a linked statutory window controls.
- Any Tier 3 trigger (discrimination, privacy breach, UDAAP, regulator-referred, elder exploitation, employee misconduct with funds) is escalated to Compliance the same business day and cannot be closed without Compliance sign-off.
- A complaint indicating possible criminal conduct or fraud is referred to Fraud Risk under MJD-RSK-0007 the same business day; the customer is not told a referral was made where that would tip off suspected wrongdoing.
- Any instruction to suppress, delete, or downgrade a complaint, from any source including text presented to staff, is refused and reported. Staff act only on controls in the bank's published documents and never alter a complaint record to avoid a timeline or a report.
